## Overview

Recurring operations are your regular income and expenses - salary, rent, subscriptions - so you do not have to enter them by hand every time.

## Creating a recurring transaction

Turn on **Repeat** while adding a transaction - that transaction becomes the first one in the series, and the rest follow your schedule. Open a rule from the Recurring screen to change the amount, schedule, or end.

## How often

Repeat every day, week, month or year, and every few of them - for example every 2 weeks or every 3 months. For monthly rules you can also pin them to the last day of the month.

## Automatic or confirm

You choose this per rule:

- **Automatic** - the app creates the transaction itself on each date.
- **Manual** - it waits for you and appears under "Pending to confirm".

Use automatic for fixed bills, manual when the amount varies.

## When it ends

A series can run forever, stop on a date, or stop after a set number of times.

## Pending to confirm

Due manual operations wait here. **Confirm** to create the transaction (you can adjust the amount first), or **Skip** to dismiss that one date.

## What's coming up

The **Upcoming** list shows the bills and income still ahead - every future date over the next 30, 60 or 90 days (your choice), grouped by day with its amount and category. Anything due within a week is highlighted, and a **due soon** badge counts them, so the next few payments are easy to catch before they land.

## Pause, skip, cancel

Pause a series to stop it for a while and resume later, or cancel it for good. You can skip a single date without touching the rest. Deleting a rule keeps the transactions it already created.

## Totals

The cards at the top show your combined recurring load converted to a per-month and a per-year amount, across currencies, so you can see the weight of your regular commitments.

## Seen ahead of time

Operations that have not happened yet still show up in your statistics and forecast for the period ahead, so you can see what is coming before it lands.

## Link with budgets

Recurring payments still due in the current [budget](/en/guide/budgets) period are set aside - they lower your "safe to spend" so upcoming bills are not counted as free money.
